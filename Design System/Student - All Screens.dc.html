KEY IDEA {{ deckNum }} / 5
{{ deckText }}
AIGenerated from this lesson · reviewed by Prof. Khanna
1. Normalize first. Rewrite every ratio so the shared term matches (LCM of the two B values).
2. Chain multipliers. Ratios compose by multiplication — never add ratio terms across statements.
3. Collapse at the end. Only reduce to lowest terms after the full chain, or you lose the common base.
At 17:20, why do we scale to B = 12 instead of 6?
TA · Meera: 12 is the LCM of 3 and 4 — with 6 you'd get fractional parts for A. Try A:B = 2:3 → 4:6 and B:C = 4:5 → 6:7.5. See the problem?
