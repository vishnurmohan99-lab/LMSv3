Why is ΔU zero for isothermal ideal gas?
NCERT says heat absorbed equals work done in isothermal expansion, which implies ΔU = 0. But the gas is absorbing heat — why doesn't its internal energy rise?
▲ 12 helpful14 replies
PK
Prof. Khanna ✓ MENTOR 1h
Internal energy of an ideal gas depends only on temperature. Isothermal means T is constant, so U can't change — every joule of heat absorbed leaves immediately as work. Real gases differ slightly (Joule–Thomson), but that's beyond NEET.
